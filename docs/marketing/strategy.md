# OKrunit Free Marketing Playbook

Prioritized by effort-to-impact ratio. Everything here is $0.

---

## Tier 1: Launch platforms (do these first, biggest bang)

### 1. Product Hunt
- **Why**: Single biggest day-one traffic source for dev tools. Top 5 product of the day can drive 2,000+ signups.
- **When**: Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday. Midnight PT start time.
- **Materials**: See `product-hunt.md`
- **Prep**: Get 5-10 people to upvote and leave comments in the first hour. Ask friends, co-founders, early users.

### 2. Hacker News (Show HN)
- **Why**: Technical audience that actually builds automation. A front-page Show HN can drive 10,000+ visits in a day.
- **When**: Weekday mornings (9-11am ET). Do NOT post on the same day as Product Hunt.
- **Materials**: See `hacker-news.md`
- **Tips**: Be genuine, don't be salesy. HN rewards technical depth and honesty about trade-offs.

### 3. Reddit
- **Why**: Subreddits like r/zapier, r/n8n, r/automation have your exact target audience.
- **When**: Space posts 2-3 days apart across different subreddits. Don't spam.
- **Materials**: See `reddit.md`
- **Rules**: Most subreddits ban self-promotion. Frame posts as "I built this" stories, not ads.

## Tier 2: Ongoing channels (build over weeks)

### 4. SEO blog content
- **Why**: Compounds over time. One good article can drive traffic for years.
- **Target keywords**:
  - "human in the loop automation" (low competition, high intent)
  - "zapier approval step" (people searching for this exact feature)
  - "make.com approval before action" (same)
  - "n8n human approval" (same)
  - "ai agent safety guardrails" (trending topic)
  - "prevent automation mistakes" (pain-point driven)
- **Materials**: See `blog-posts.md` for 3 ready-to-publish articles.

### 5. Twitter/X and LinkedIn
- **Why**: Free, compounds with consistency. Twitter for dev audience, LinkedIn for enterprise buyers.
- **Cadence**: 3-5 tweets per week, 2 LinkedIn posts per week.
- **Materials**: See `social-media.md` for 20+ ready-to-post drafts.

### 6. Integration marketplaces
- **Why**: People searching "approval" inside Zapier/Make/n8n are ready to buy.
- **Materials**: See `marketplace-listings.md`
- **Actions**:
  - Zapier: Submit your app to the Zapier App Directory (zapier.com/developer)
  - Make: Publish your app in the Make marketplace
  - n8n: Your community node is already on npm. Add it to the n8n community node list.

## Tier 3: Outreach (do when you have time)

### 7. Cold email to automation agencies
- **Why**: Agencies build automations for dozens of clients. One agency = many customers.
- **Materials**: See `outreach.md`
- **Where to find them**: Zapier Expert directory, Make Partner directory, Upwork automation freelancers.

### 8. YouTube tutorials
- **Why**: "How to add approval to Zapier" has almost no competition on YouTube.
- **Effort**: Record a 5-min screen share. No editing needed.
- **Topics**:
  - "Add human approval to any Zapier Zap in 2 minutes"
  - "Prevent AI agents from doing dangerous things"
  - "How I stopped an automation from deleting 10,000 records"

### 9. Community engagement
- **Where**:
  - n8n Community Forum (community.n8n.io) -- post in "Share your workflow"
  - Make Community (community.make.com)
  - Zapier Community (community.zapier.com)
  - Automation subreddit Discord servers
  - AI safety/alignment communities (LessWrong, Alignment Forum)
  - Indie Hackers (indiehackers.com)
- **Approach**: Answer questions, share workflows, link to OKrunit when relevant.

### 10. Dev.to and Hashnode articles
- **Why**: Free blogging platforms with built-in developer audience.
- **Republish**: Take your SEO blog posts and cross-post them here with a canonical link back to okrunit.com/blog.
